Following the money, two hops out

Funds leave an exchange, scatter across fresh withdrawal addresses, then quietly converge on a single wallet. That convergence is a signature of scam and mule networks. This is how we detect it — and how far we can take it.

⚡
The point
This is flag and investigate, not compliance. The strongest predictor of who gets scammed next is who got scammed before — so surfacing consolidation early lets you protect the same people twice.

What else we can do

Two hops is the starting point, not the limit. Reach deeper into the network, narrow the time window, raise the bar for what counts as convergence, or layer in a velocity rule — funds that arrive and leave fast.

Patterns we can build with you
🌀
Convergence

Many exchange withdrawals funnelling onto one wallet — the core two-hop signal shown above.

⏱️
Rapid pass-through

Funds arrive and ≥95% leaves within a week. Money that doesn't rest is money being moved on, not held.

🪜
Deeper layering

Three or four hops instead of two, to follow flows that take an extra step to shake off the trail.

🔀
Fan-out, fan-in

One source sprays across many addresses, then everything reconverges — splitting to look clean, then regrouping.

🤝
Built around your users

The right thresholds depend on how your users actually transact. We co-design the hop depth, time windows and velocity rules to fit your base — so what surfaces is worth an investigator's time, and the people most likely to be hit again get protected first.
